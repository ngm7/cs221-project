“The Artists Who Brought Asian-Americans Into the Annals of Contemporary Art” by Nikil Saval

As part of our early ’80s-themed Culture issue, Nikil Saval reflected on the influence of the Asian-American artists Tseng Kwong Chi and Martin Wong and what it’s truly like to be an immigrant or a child of immigrants in this country. Saval writes: “In the early 1980s, that spellbound interregnum of American life, when high interest rates were turning the screws on the American working class, just before the lurid depredations of the Reagan era took hold, Tseng and Wong dramatized the ‘permanent outsider’ status that afflicts Asian-Americans to this day, and restaged it to their advantage.” Read more.
