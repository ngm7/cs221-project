“A Brief History of Gay Theater, in Three Acts” by Jesse Green

The Times’ co-chief theater critic Jesse Green wrote about the return of “The Boys in the Band” and why it matters more than ever for our spring Men’s Fashion issue. The play by Mart Crowley, about nine gay men at a birthday party, had its Off Broadway premiere in April 1968 and reopened on Broadway at the end of May — “an acknowledgment of a larger urgency about the representation of gay men in popular entertainment: a moment that, in the theater at least, is both springboard and eulogy,” Green explains. “At a time when many of the classic gay plays are returning to the Broadway stage — ‘Boys in the Band,’ ‘Angels in America’ and ‘Torch Song Trilogy’ among them — almost no new ones are on the horizon to join them.” Read more.
