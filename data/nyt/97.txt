Good Monday morning. Here are some of the stories making news in Washington and politics today.

_____________________

• The United States and China reached a truce in the escalating trade war. The compromise struck Saturday by President Trump and President Xi Jinping was a reprieve for both leaders after the fight had inflicted economic damage on both sides. But it offers few promises of policy changes.

• Lawmakers expected tough negotiations over a spending package, with a government shutdown possible if no deal could be reached by Friday. But after the death of former President George Bush, they are discussing a short-term bill that delays the deadline for one or two weeks.

• The spirit of comity fostered by the shared mourning of Mr. Bush will almost certainly dissipate within days of his funeral, our correspondent writes, underlining how much Washington, and the nation, have changed since his days.
