From common weeds like the wild dandelion and the formerly despised anthurium — also known as “penis on a platter,” as Deborah Needleman wrote in her story about the resurgence of ’80s floral arrangements — many previously unfashionable flowers came back into vogue this year. Accordingly, the pages of T were filled with unexpected arrangements, including clouds of Queen Anne’s lace, swathes of Japanese knotweed and vases of rainbow-colored baby’s breath, pastel-dyed orchids and flamboyant birds of paradise.
